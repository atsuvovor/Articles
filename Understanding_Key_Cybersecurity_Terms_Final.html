Understanding Key Cybersecurity Terms and Risk Factors

Term Definition Examples/Notes
Risk The likelihood that bad events happen. -
Issue When appropriate action is not taken to limit the given risk. -
Incident When something bad has happened, or the risk scenario becomes reality. -
Risk Finding Proactively identifying the issue and documenting it. -
KPIs Focus on measuring security control effectiveness and operational success. Incident response time, training completion rate, vulnerability remediation rate.
KRIs Focus on indicators of potential risk and exposure to threats. Data breaches, phishing success rates, third-party risk compliance.
Threat The potential for a malicious actor to infect the organizationâ€™s systems. Represents the likelihood or frequency of a potential event exploiting a vulnerability.
  • Threat Identification: Using algorithms on past incidents (threat intelligence feeds) to anticipate attacks.
  • Potential Attacks: Phishing, ransomware, unauthorized access, DoS attacks.
Vulnerability The extent to which a system, network, or process is susceptible to being exploited. Example: Outdated email filtering software rated 0.6 (60%).
Impact The magnitude of potential damage if the vulnerability is exploited. Example: Successful phishing attack causing $500,000 loss, rated at 500,000 monetary units.

Cybersecurity Key Risk Factors (KRFs)

Formula:

Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact

Sample Risk Scenarios

Scenario Threat Likelihood Vulnerability Level Impact Value Risk Calculation Notes
High Threat, Moderate Vulnerability, High Impact 0.7 0.6 $500,000 0.7 × 0.6 × 500,000 = 210,000 The calculated risk is $210,000, representing potential cost if the threat materializes.
Low Threat, High Vulnerability, Moderate Impact 0.2 0.8 $100,000 0.2 × 0.8 × 100,000 = 16,000 Low risk due to low likelihood despite high vulnerability.
High Threat, Low Vulnerability, High Impact 0.9 0.3 $1,000,000 0.9 × 0.3 × 1,000,000 = 270,000 Strong defenses reduce the overall risk despite high threat and impact.
